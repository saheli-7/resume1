Minimalism Architecture
Minimalism in architecture is a design approach that emphasizes simplicity, clarity, and the reduction of elements to their essential forms. It emerged as a reaction to the ornate and overly decorative styles that dominated architecture in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Minimalist architecture is characterized by its clean lines, neutral color palettes, and focus on functionality.
  • Simplicity: Minimalist architecture seeks to eliminate unnecessary ornamentation and complexity, allowing the inherent beauty of basic shapes and materials to shine through.

  • Clean Lines: Straight lines, right angles, and geometric shapes are common in minimalist designs. These elements contribute to a sense of order and balance in the overall composition.
