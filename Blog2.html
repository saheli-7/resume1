Modern Architecture
It seems like you might be referring to "Modern Architecture," which is a style of architecture that emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a response to traditional architectural styles and the changing societal and technological context of the time. Modern Architecture is characterized by a focus on functionality, simplicity, and the use of new materials and construction techniques. Here are some key points about Modern Architecture:
  • Functionalism: Modern architecture emphasizes the functional aspects of a building. Form follows function, which means that the design of a building should be determined by its intended purpose and use.

  • Simplicity: Modern architecture rejects ornate and decorative elements in favor of clean lines, minimalism, and simplicity in design. This approach is often summarized by the famous phrase "less is more.
